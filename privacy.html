This Privacy Policy explains how Future One Group Pty Ltd (trading as Trusio) handles personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). It also covers how we handle credit-related information in accordance with the Privacy (Credit Reporting) Code 2014.

By using the Trusio platform (trusio.com.au and app.trusio.com.au), you agree to the practices described in this policy.

1. Information We Collect

Information about Business Users (Suppliers / "B" users)

  • Name, email address, and company details
  • Login credentials and account preferences
  • Credit policy settings and configuration
  • Usage data and activity logs within the platform

Information about Credit Applicants ("A1" users — Business)

  • Company name, ABN, and registered business details
  • Director name and contact information
  • Trade reference details
  • Credit application information and supporting documents
  • Personal Guarantee signatory details and electronic signature

Information about Individual Credit Applicants ("A1" users — Consumer)

  • Full name, date of birth, and home address
  • Email address and mobile number
  • Driver licence number (optional)
  • Electronic signature and consent records
  • Consumer credit report information (obtained with consent)

2. How We Collect Information

We collect information in the following ways:

  • Directly from you — when you register, complete a credit application, or sign a consent form on our platform
  • From third-party credit reporting bodies — such as InfoTrackGO or CreditorWatch, where you have provided consent for us to obtain a credit report
  • From the Australian Business Register — for ABN and company verification purposes
  • From ASIC — for company registration and director verification
  • Automatically — through cookies and usage data when you use our platform

3. How We Use Your Information

We use personal information for the following purposes:

  • To provide the Trusio credit onboarding platform and its features
  • To verify the identity and creditworthiness of credit applicants
  • To facilitate the credit assessment process between suppliers and their customers
  • To generate and store Personal Guarantee documents
  • To send transactional emails (application links, consent requests, approval notifications)
  • To comply with our legal obligations under Australian law
  • To improve and develop our platform

We do not use your personal information for direct marketing without your express consent.

4. Disclosure of Your Information

We may disclose your personal information to:

  • The supplier using Trusio — who has invited you to complete a credit application. They will receive your credit assessment results and application details.
  • Credit reporting bodies — to obtain a consumer or commercial credit report where consent has been provided
  • Service providers — including Supabase (database), Resend (email delivery), and Vercel (hosting), who are bound by confidentiality obligations
  • Law enforcement or government agencies — where required by law

We do not sell your personal information to third parties.

⚠️ Note for individual applicants: Your credit report and personal information will be shared with the business (supplier) that requested the credit check. By completing the consent form on our platform, you authorise this disclosure.

5. Credit Reporting Information

Where we collect credit reporting information about individuals, we do so in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Privacy (Credit Reporting) Code 2014.

Consumer Credit Information

We only collect consumer credit information about an individual where that individual has provided written consent via our platform's consent form. This consent authorises:

  • The supplier using Trusio to obtain a consumer credit report from a credit reporting body
  • The use of that report to assess the individual's creditworthiness

What Credit Information May Include

  • Credit history and repayment history
  • Defaults and overdue payments
  • Court judgments and insolvency information
  • Credit enquiries

Your Rights Regarding Credit Information

You have the right to:

  • Request access to credit information held about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate credit information
  • Make a complaint if you believe your credit information has been mishandled

6. Security

We take reasonable steps to protect your personal information from misuse, interference, loss, and unauthorised access. Our security measures include:

  • Encrypted data storage via Supabase (PostgreSQL with row-level security)
  • HTTPS encryption for all data in transit
  • Access controls limiting data access to authorised users only
  • Regular security reviews of our platform and infrastructure

Despite these measures, no internet transmission is completely secure. We encourage you to protect your account credentials and notify us immediately if you suspect any unauthorised access.

7. Access and Correction

You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you, and to request correction of any information that is inaccurate, out of date, or incomplete.

To make a request, contact us at hello@trusio.com.au. We will respond within 30 days. In some cases, we may be unable to provide access (for example, where it would reveal information about another person), and we will explain why.

8. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to provide our services and comply with our legal obligations:

  • Credit applications and related documents — 7 years from the date of the application, in accordance with Australian record-keeping requirements
  • Personal Guarantee documents — retained for the duration of the credit relationship plus 7 years
  • Account information — retained while your account is active and for 2 years after closure
  • Consent records — retained for 7 years as evidence of lawful collection of credit information

When information is no longer required, we will securely delete or de-identify it.

9. Complaints

If you believe we have breached the Australian Privacy Principles or the Privacy (Credit Reporting) Code, you may make a complaint by contacting us at hello@trusio.com.au.

We will acknowledge your complaint within 5 business days and aim to resolve it within 30 days. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may refer your complaint to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at oaic.gov.au or by calling 1300 363 992.
